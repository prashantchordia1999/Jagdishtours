The Falcon report

The city hall tower is home to two peregrine falcons - Clara and her current tiercel, Esteban Colbert! Esteban is named after Stephen Colbert, a TV personality who San Jose’s Mayor is said to be a fan of.

A mystery maze house

The legend says Mrs. Winchester was afraid of ghosts of those killed by the Winchester rifle so she insisted her Victorian house be kept under constant construction for 38 years. Continuous adding of doors, secret passageways and stairways that lead nowhere were methods for confusing evil spirits and the result is a labyrinth that can keep you busy for hours.

Organic paradise

Californian food is heavily influenced by Latin cooking and is based on fresh shellfish, seafood, local organic produce and fusion cuisine. Crab Louie salad is a typical meal you can enjoy there.

Tech heaven

With over 6,600 technology companies, San Jose is the most densely concentrated technology area in the world.

Real estate kingdom in the park

Who says you have to stay in to play board games? San Jose’s Guadalupe River Park has a larger than life Monopoly board! It’s 30 square feet, making it the biggest Monopoly board in the world.

Babe, is that you?

Babe the Muffler Man is the last of the three fiberglass giants that used to overlook San Jose. Made popular in the 60’s by a fiberglass company, Muffler Men can be seen scattered beside California’s highways.

Sinking ghost town

Once called Saline City, Drawbridge is a ghost town on Station Island. Founded in 1876 and abandoned in 1979, today it is slowly sinking into the marshlands.
