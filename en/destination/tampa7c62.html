Exotic fusion

Tampa’s signature Floribbean cuisine is a mix of Latin American and Caribbean gastronomy. The famous Cuban sandwich actually originates from Tampa and it’s the city’s official sandwich.

Cuban sandwich

  • 4 medium-large slices crusty bread
  • 2 tablespoons yellow mustard
  • 8 thin slices Swiss cheese
  • 8 thick slices crispy roasted pork
  • 8 thin slices smoked ham
  • 4 large pickle slices
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter

Guava city

Because of the historical importance of the tropical fruit guava, Tampa goes by the nickname 'Big Guava'. Tampans enjoy guava jelly, guava paste, guava turnovers and pickled guavas.

Pirate ambush!

Tampa is the only city that gets attacked by pirates every year. The Gasparilla Pirate Festival celebrates the legend of a mythical Spanish pirate who once raided the coast of West Florida.

Sangria heaven

750 bottles of wine and 226 kg of fruit were used to make the one tonne drink and set a new world record for the largest pitcher of sangria!

Cigar capital

One of Tampa’s neighbourhoods was once known as the Cigar Capital of the World. At its peak, Ybor City’s cigar makers produced about 700 million cigars a year.
