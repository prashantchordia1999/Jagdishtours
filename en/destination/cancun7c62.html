Spice it up

The cuisine of Cancun is a modern mix of Mexican delicacies and specialities from the rest of the world. Huevos rancheros is a traditional breakfast that encapsulates the essence of Mexican gastronomy.

Huevos rancheros

  • 5 tbs olive oil
  • 1 small whole onion (peeled and chopped)
  • 1 tbs ground cumin
  • 12 ounces of black beans (drained if using from a can)
  • 4 ounce enchilada sauce
  • 2 corn tortillas
  • 2-4 eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese

Art under the sea

It’s not just caves that are underwater. In Cancun, there’s also an entire museum! In Museo Subacuático de Arte, guests can see more than 450 life-size sculptures at the bottom of the Caribbean.

Allure of the coral

Cancun is the home of the second largest coral reef in the world, the Great Mayan Barrier Reef. Visitors have an exclusive opportunity to witness its beauty on scuba diving and snorkelling trips.

Law and order (a tequila)

Mexicans care about tequila so much they have a law that strictly defines how tequila is to be produced. To be in accordance with this law, at least 51% of every tequila must come from the blue agave plant.

I now pronounce you...

Cancun is a Mexican oasis of affection. Every year, more than 46,000 couples exchange vows and pledge their eternal love to each other.
